My Videogames

> Jim The Cactus

This platform game is about Jim, an humble cactus trying to fight against worm-like enemies and reach new worlds from one gate to the next. Help Jim overcome his journey and collect as many gems as possible! Once your player gets enough green gems, unlock new superpowers and have fun with Jim!

This content runs on the web, click or tap and play!




> Dona Game 2

"Dona Game 2" is the second version of the R. Donatelli high school video game. It's an educational project designed to show the everyday life inside the institute to new pupils. This bizarre and revolutionary enviroment let you explore odd places and virtual classrooms where you can either listen to intriguing teachers' explanations or try solving physic or latin questions and problems!
Authors: Giulia Rossi, Giuli Neri, Matilde Piacentini, Simone Amici and me.

> Rebellion King

Rebellion King is one of the video games which all began with. You play the role of a foolish king sentenced to death by his subjects. The wrath of your peasents laids on you, king of all times and lands, will you survive? Flee as far as you can from those human devils, pick potions you find up to ghost yourself, slow farmes down until your time comes. A sword, it's time to struggle!

Download the Android game on Aptoide and leave a review.




> Tap Bird

A personal reinterpretation of 'Flappy bird' developed in order to entice other students of my institute to videogame development first. My other applications

> PicturesExtractor (C for Linux)
> PicturesExtractor (C for Mac)

A command-line program able to randomly select the desired number of files - any type - from a specific directory and then copy them to another one.

First, compile the C source file on your machine. Second, invoke the program after compiling it, place your current directory in the folder containing the executable and write the following line in your shell with the right substitution: ./PicturesExtractor 'source_dir' 'dest_dir' 'number_of_files'.




> FCards (.exe for Windows x64)
> FCards (.app for Mac x64)

A flashcards app based on Excel files. You can fill the front and the back of your card and push it in your deck. When your new deck is ready or old one loaded from your file system, select the sorting order and surf among your words and expressions.

Choose your version depending on your machine. Please consider switching your antivirus off in case of odd behaviours.
